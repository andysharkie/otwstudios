Prestige Cabinetry.

Thirty years behind Perth's finest homes, and a website that did not say so.

Client
Prestige Cabinetry Perth
Role
Brand, Web
Year
2026
Stack
Static build, CMS-editable
01 — Setup

Thirty years, and no shopfront for it.

Prestige Cabinetry Perth is a father-and-son workshop in Osborne Park. Mark and Michael Barrett design, build, and install every piece themselves. Mark has spent over thirty years running cabinet-making on high-end projects, and is well known among Perth's builders and architects.

The reputation lived in those relationships. But a builder or architect new to Prestige had nowhere to look. No website that showed the work, told the story, or made the quality obvious before the first meeting.

02 — Brief

Win the trade.

Build a site that earns the trust of the people who specify high-end cabinetry: builders, architects, and homeowners with serious projects. Make the father-and-son model the selling point, not a footnote. And make the standard of the work obvious on first read.

Three things had to be true. It had to look like the cabinetry. It had to speak to the trade. And it had to make clear that, with Prestige, you deal directly with the two people making your work.

03 — Approach

To the millimetre.

Restraint that reads as quality. Warm timber tones, generous space, and editorial typography. A site as considered as the joinery, with nothing shouting over the work.

Built for the trade. The language speaks to builders and architects: to the drawing, to the millimetre, to the deadline. The pitch is direct access to the makers, with no middleman and no junior rushing a finish.

The work, up front. Real projects across Perth's best suburbs, each one shown the way a specifier wants to see it.

Bespoke, measured to the millimetre, in your home.

04 — Build

A site as considered as the joinery.

A fast static build, CMS-editable, designed so Mark and Michael can add new projects as they finish. Underneath the surface, the site is built to convert the trade.

  • iBrand and website. An identity and a site covering the story, the full range of what they build, recent work, and a clear path to discuss a project.
  • iiBuilt for builders and architects. Structured around how the trade specifies work, with the father-and-son model as the core advantage.
  • iiiEditable by the makers. A simple editor so the workshop can grow the portfolio without a developer.

A handful of recent jobs, shown the way a specifier wants to see them.

Excellent workmanship, and excellent service. The only way they know how to work.

05 — Result

A shopfront for the workshop.

Prestige now has a site that matches the cabinetry, speaks the language of the trade, and makes the father-and-son advantage the headline. Thirty years of work finally has somewhere a new builder or architect can see it.

The two people making your work.
